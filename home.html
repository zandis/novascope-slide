The Thesis

Why Semiconductors Will Transform Diagnostics

In 1971, Intel put 2,300 transistors on a single chip and launched the microprocessor revolution. Within three decades, semiconductors transformed computing, communications, and finance. The mechanism was always the same: miniaturize onto silicon, mass-produce in a foundry, and make it cheap enough for the world.

One of the last great holdouts is medical diagnostics. The $80+ billion IVD industry still runs on the same physics it used forty years ago: optics. A BioFire test costs $150–$180. A Quanterix SIMOA instrument costs $300,000–$400,000. These are not tools for primary care clinics.

Novascope was founded on a different premise: the right physics for diagnostics is not light — it is electricity.

Every biomolecule carries an electric charge. A field-effect transistor detects changes in charge with extraordinary precision. FETs are the most manufactured, most reliable, most scalable objects in human history. We built the complete semiconductor-grade system to turn this principle into a clinical-grade diagnostic platform.

From 8-inch semiconductor wafer to individual biochip — each containing billions of probe sensor sites
