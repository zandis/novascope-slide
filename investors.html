Market Catalyst

Why Now: Three Converging Forces

Three macro forces are creating a once-in-a-generation opening for a semiconductor-based diagnostics platform.

1.27M

AMR Crisis

Deaths annually from antimicrobial resistance (WHO/Lancet 2019 Global Burden Study). $24B US hospital cost. 7.6% mortality increase per hour of delayed treatment (Kumar et al., Crit Care Med 2006).

Current culture-based testing: 2–7 days. Novascope: <60 minutes from whole blood.

$26.5K

AD Drug Revolution

Annual cost of lecanemab (Leqembi). Every patient considered for anti-amyloid therapy requires biomarker confirmation. The screening infrastructure does not exist at current price points.

Current: $500–$1,250/test at reference labs. Novascope target: ~$200/test at local clinics.

~$10

Semiconductor Advantage

Target COGS per test at scale. First bio-fabless company as direct customer of UMC. Semiconductor cost curves: every 2× volume, costs drop 25–30%.

BioFire: $150–$180/test. SIMOA instrument: $300K–$400K. Novascope: desktop platform.

The Thesis

Investment Thesis

1

Platform Value, Not Product Value

The chip doesn’t care what molecule it detects. Every new application requires only new probes and assay protocol development — the chip, reader, software, and manufacturing infrastructure are reusable. Value accrues to the platform, not any single test.

2

End-to-End IP Ownership

40+ patents covering chip design, surface chemistry, antibody engineering, reader hardware, and AI analytics. Zero external IP dependencies. Full vertical integration from wafer to clinical result.

3

Semiconductor Cost Curves

Target ~$10 COGS vs. $150–$180 for BioFire. Fabricated at UMC as a direct customer — every 2× volume drives 25–30% cost reduction. The same economics that gave us $0.10 transistors will give us $10 diagnostic tests.
