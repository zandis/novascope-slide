Opportunity

We don’t make tests. We make the semiconductor platform that makes tests possible. Series B biotech raising $10–15M at $80M post-money valuation.

The Thesis

Platform Value, Not Product Value

An IVD company develops assays, licenses instruments, and sells kits. Its value is the sum of its approved products.

A BioSemiconductor company designs the foundational chip technology, owns the complete IP stack, and builds application-specific systems on a reusable platform. Its value is the platform itself — the accumulated know-how that enables any new application to be built in months, not years.

“The chip doesn’t care what molecule it detects. Any electrically charged molecule captured by a surface-bound probe generates a detectable signal. Every new application requires only new probes and assay protocol development. The chip, reader, software, and manufacturing infrastructure are reusable.”

The platform: from bare chip to unlimited diagnostic applications

Why Now: Three Converging Forces

1.27M

AMR Crisis

Deaths annually from antimicrobial resistance (WHO/Lancet 2019 Global Burden Study). $24B US hospital cost. 7.6% mortality increase per hour of delayed treatment (Kumar et al., Crit Care Med 2006).

Current culture-based testing: 2–7 days. Novascope: <60 minutes from whole blood.

$26.5K

AD Drug Revolution

Annual cost of lecanemab (Leqembi). Every patient considered for anti-amyloid therapy requires biomarker confirmation. The screening infrastructure does not exist at current price points.

Current: $500–$1,250/test at reference labs. Novascope target: ~$200/test at local clinics.

~$10

Semiconductor Advantage

Target COGS per test at scale. First bio-fabless company as direct customer of UMC. Semiconductor cost curves: every 2× volume, costs drop 25–30%.

BioFire: $150–$180/test. SIMOA instrument: $300K–$400K. Novascope: desktop platform.
